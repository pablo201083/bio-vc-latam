# Project Context From Pitch

This note captures the strategic frame that should guide the BIO VC LATAM mapping work.

## Core Thesis

The project is not only a startup database. It is ecosystem intelligence infrastructure for helping the Latin American BIO protoecosystem become more coordinated, legible, investable, and connected to industrial demand.

BIO is understood broadly as a general-purpose technology for transforming the material base of the economy. The relevant universe includes startups that are bio-based, biocentric, or directly connected to planetary-boundary management through life-centered or material-transition logic.

## Strategic Opportunity

Argentina and LATAM sit at the convergence of four currents:

- Long scientific capability-building in life sciences and related fields.
- A maturing generation of science-based startups and deep-tech funds.
- Regional abundance in biodiversity, biomass, land, food systems, and natural assets.
- A new technological base combining biology, sequencing, molecular design, automation, data, and AI.

The opportunity is to turn scattered niches into an ecosystem platform with global relevance.

## Main Bottlenecks

- Fragmentation: the ecosystem is split across verticals such as agtech, biotech, healthtech, foodtech, climate, and materials.
- Low legibility: investors, corporates, and public actors cannot easily see who exists, what they do, how they connect, or where the strongest opportunities are.
- Underinvestment: capital is not reaching the scale needed to move from scientific disruption to market maturation.
- Weak adoption pathways: startups often struggle to connect with corporates, early adopters, pilots, validation, and scale partners.
- Insufficient regional critical mass: Argentina matters, but BIO often requires a Southern Cone / LATAM field to reach enough talent, demand, capital, and institutional density.

## Intervention Logic

The project should operate as an integrated flywheel:

- Map: build a high-quality, auditable, versioned data layer of startups, funds, corporates, institutions, and relationships.
- Understand: convert the data into analytical products, taxonomy, cluster logic, bottleneck diagnosis, and investment/adoption insight.
- Connect: use the intelligence layer to support investor-startup, corporate-startup, and ecosystem-actor matching.
- Amplify: transform evidence and cases into a shared identity and credible international narrative for BIO LATAM.

## Implications For This Repository

- Data quality is strategic, not cosmetic. Source-backed startup descriptions and public/auditable relationship evidence are central to trust.
- Include/exclude decisions should be defensible against the broad BIO thesis, not just conventional sector labels.
- The capital network should become a first-class product view, not a side export.
- Taxonomy should help outsiders understand the ecosystem quickly while preserving nuance for edge cases.
- Visualizations should answer real ecosystem questions: where capital concentrates, which themes are underfunded, who bridges clusters, where countries or funds specialize, and where the map is still incomplete.
- The system should support future KPIs: coverage, documented relationships, source-backed share, capital mobilized, pilots/adoption links, regional density, and strategic visibility.

## Product North Star

Make BIO LATAM recognizable, searchable, analyzable, investable, and actionable.
